## Earlier Career — Quality Assurance

### QA Development Manager — EA (2005)

**QA Central Services — Online + Compliance**

Managed cross-platform QA testing teams responsible for Online and Compliance QA for numerous EA titles during the online gaming revolution. Oversaw test efforts across multiple titles and platforms, from PC to PS2 and PSP, and also to the launch of Xbox Online and Xbox 360.

During that time, my testers proved their metal on NFS Underground 2 + Most Wanted, EA Sports Online Titles: FIFA, Madden, Tiger Woods, NBA, NHL, NCAA, and shooters like Medal of Honor and Time Splitters (half my team was sent to UK for 2 months!). And, two of my testers met on the project and eventually got married!

**Submission Audit Team**

Tasked to create and lead the Submission Audit Team to pre-test projects and identify critical and costly issues ahead of certification. For this, I built a team of specialized testers from the ranks of EA QA, analyzed EA's historical data on third-party and first-party quality certification, built a test plan, and generated a submission confidence report for each project.

### QA Tester — Online Clean Room (2004)

Worked with EA IT to provision a clean room space for online performance testing EA's titles using network emulation tools and real-world internet. I was essentially off-grid in a special room inside of EA where I tested all of EA's online-capable titles.

I worked directly with other studio testing departments to handle games they were not equipped for (Burnout, Fight Night, etc.), worked directly with online developers to resolve performance issues, consulted with certification groups on router and port configuration issues, helped EA Singapore set up their own clean room, and built special relationships with EA's central online dev teams.

### QA Tester — EA Sports Online (2003)

Got my start in EA QA testing the full suite of EA Sports online titles — FIFA, Madden, NBA Live, NHL, and NCAA — during the early days of console online play. I verified online connectivity, matchmaking, and head-to-head features across platforms, surfacing the kinds of network and performance issues that led directly into the dedicated online clean room work that followed.
